The History of Lasagna

Etymology

The term "lasagna" could originate from the existence of a similar dish in Ancient Rome, called lasana or lasanum, Latin for 'container' or 'pot', but the word could have an older origin. It could also come from the Greek word laganon, which was a flat sheet of pasta dough cut into strips. As with other pasta dishes, the plural form of the word -lasagna- is used in Italian, meaning more than one sheet of lasagna. But in reference tot he baked dish, regional usage in Italy favors the plural form in the north and the singular lasagna in the south. The plural usage has influenced its spelling in British English, while the southern Italian singular usage has influenced the form used in American English.

Origin

Lasagna originated in Italy during the Middle Ages, and is referenced for the first time in a ballad from 1282. The first known recipe for lasagna was created in the early 14th century in the Libro de Coquina, and bore only a slight resemblance to the modern dish, as it was made with flattened fermented dough and cheese which was boiled and sprinkled with cheese and spices.
